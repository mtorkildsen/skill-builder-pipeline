# Generate Proposal

Create a professional business proposal document tailored to your client and opportunity.

## Before we start

I'll guide you through creating a proposal. Answer these questions to customize the output:

**Question 1: What type of proposal?**

1. **Services proposal** - Outline services you're offering (consulting, development, design)
2. **Project proposal** - Detailed plan for a specific project with timeline and deliverables
3. **Product proposal** - Present a product and its business value
4. **Pricing proposal** - Quote for work or products with cost breakdown

Or describe: If none of these fit, describe your proposal type.

**Question 2: What's the primary goal?**

1. **Win a contract** - Persuade client to hire you (focus on value, timeline, team)
2. **Get feedback** - Request input before full commitment (focus on approach, alternatives)
3. **Document an agreement** - Formalize understood terms (focus on clarity, specificity)

Or describe: Share what you're trying to achieve if it's different.

**Question 3: Output format preference?**

1. **Word (.docx)** - Professional formatting, easy to edit, email-friendly
2. **PDF** - Locked layout, no editing, looks polished when shared
3. **Google Docs** - Collaborative, web-accessible, easy to comment

Or type: Let me know if you have another preference.

## Proposal information

Once you've answered the initial questions, provide:

### Client details (required)
- Client name
- Contact person (if known)
- Industry/business type

### Proposal content (required)
- What are you proposing? (2-3 sentence summary)
- Why should they accept? (main value proposition)
- Key deliverables or scope (bullet list)
- Timeline (phases, milestones, or completion date)
- Investment/pricing (if appropriate for the stage)

### Optional details
- Your company background
- Team members and their roles
- Past relevant experience
- Success metrics or KPIs
- Terms and conditions

## Proposal structure

I'll create a proposal with these sections:

```
1. Executive Summary
   - One-paragraph overview of the proposal

2. Problem Statement
   - What challenge are you solving?

3. Proposed Solution
   - Your approach and how it addresses the problem

4. Deliverables & Timeline
   - What you'll deliver and when

5. Investment
   - Cost breakdown and payment terms

6. Why Us
   - Your qualifications and relevant experience

7. Next Steps
   - How they move forward with you
```

Sections will be customized based on your proposal type.

## Interaction pattern: Customization loop

After I generate the initial proposal, you can:

**Option A: Approve and download**
- "This looks good, generate the document"
- I'll create the .docx, .pdf, or share the Google Doc

**Option B: Modify specific sections**
- "Make the timeline longer" → I'll adjust
- "Add details about our team" → I'll expand
- "Rewrite the problem statement" → I'll reframe

**Option C: Change direction**
- "Actually, this is a project proposal not services" → I'll restructure
- "Add cost breakdown details" → I'll detail the pricing
- "Make it shorter" → I'll condense to essentials

**Option D: Need help deciding**
- "I'm not sure about the timeline" → I'll show options
- "How much should we charge?" → I'll research and suggest
- "What should the scope include?" → I'll recommend standard scope

## Common proposal patterns

### Quick proposal (2-3 pages)
Use for: Simple services, clear scope
Time to create: 30 minutes
Include: Problem, solution, investment, next steps

### Comprehensive proposal (8-10 pages)
Use for: Major projects, multiple stakeholders
Time to create: 2 hours
Include: All sections, team details, case studies

### Visual proposal (5-7 pages with graphics)
Use for: Design/creative work, visual industries
Time to create: 3 hours
Include: Mockups, timelines with graphics, visual hierarchy

## Tips for strong proposals

**Structure:** Clear sections with headings make proposals scannable
**Brevity:** 2-3 pages is standard; cut unnecessary details
**Specificity:** "Increase sales by 20%" beats "improve performance"
**Social proof:** Include relevant case studies or testimonials
**Clear CTA:** Make it obvious how they should respond
**Professional tone:** Confident but not arrogant; friendly but not casual

## What happens after generation

4. **Follow up** - Check in after a few days if no response
